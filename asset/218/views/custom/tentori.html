A chef loved and revered by Chicago, he was born and raised in a small town outside of Milan. At just 19 years old, he received an invitation to learn from Gabriel Viti and work in his restaurant in Highland Park. From there, Giuseppe spent 9 years honing his skills and philosophy at the famous Charlie Trotter's, creating complex dishes that excite, but don't overwhelm the diner.

Today Chef Giuseppe Tentori is kept exceptionally busy. He's the executive chef at GT Fish and Oyster, earning three stars from the Chicago Tribune and Chicago Sun Times.

Giuseppe Tentori’s recipe

no claw

Don’t be intimidated by the prep work when grilling king crab legs. Giuseppe recommends the easier route. When ordering, ask you Mariano’s butcher for "no claw." They prep it just right so it’s ready to grill.

the Big Green Egg

When grilling at home, Chef Giuseppe chooses the Big Green Egg grill, a domed ceramic cooker with a temperature control. "It’s a great grill. It’s A++. Its versatility as a grill or smoker makes it great."

chicken from the case

I like chicken in the case better because the skin is a little more dry. Not moist like in a bag. It will make the chicken skin a little crispier.

Q & A

Which Mariano’s do you shop?

Mariano’s Roscoe Village

How did chef Gabriel Viti convince you to come to the US?

He didn’t have to convince me. He asked and I said yes. I was 19 at the time.

How do you tell when a steak is done by touch only?

Lots and lots of practice. If you’re a beginner start by using a thermometer.

What’s the best way to cook a common fish like Tilapia on the grill?

With an herb and chili marinade, on a super hot grill (so the fish doesn’t stick).

What is your favorite section of Mariano’s?

I love the produce section.

Is there a surprising meal you like to cook at home?

Homemade gnocchi with marinara, fresh mozzarella, and basil.

How do you balance out complicated dishes so well?

With texture, acid, and creativity.

Why should you, or shouldn’t you chew oysters?

It depends on the size of the oyster. If it’s your first oyster, you probably don’t want to chew it.

When did you start tailgating? Do you do it often?

10 years ago at Soldier Field. Unfortunately, I don’t do it as often as I’d like.

Give us your ideal Chicago bratwurst creation. Toppings and all.

Boiled in a stout, grilled, toasted bun, caramelized onions, homemade mustard. Then eat it like cookie monster... nom nom nom.
